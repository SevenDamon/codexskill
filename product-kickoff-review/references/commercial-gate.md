# Commercial Gate

Use this before PRD, UI, docs, or code. The goal is to decide whether an idea is likely to become a paid, repeatable product.

## Core Stance

Do not make art pieces. Do not make toys. Make products that help customers save money, make money, reduce risk, or create measurable operational leverage.

## The Three Business Questions

### 1. Will anyone pay?

Answer concretely:

- Who is the paying customer?
- Who is the end user?
- Is the buyer already spending money or labor on this problem?
- What is the current workaround?
- What pain is severe enough to make them change behavior?
- What would make them say "I want to try this now" instead of "interesting"?

Pass condition:

- A specific buyer exists.
- The buyer has a current cost, risk, or revenue opportunity.
- The product has a clear path to replacing or improving that workflow.

Fail signals:

- The answer is "students", "everyone", "creators", or "small businesses" without a specific buyer.
- The value is mainly "cool", "fun", "AI-powered", or "more convenient".
- No current budget, labor cost, or operational pain is identified.

### 2. At what price?

Answer with a simple value equation:

```text
Customer value created >= 2x price
```

Estimate:

- Current monthly or annual cost.
- Time saved.
- Labor replaced or amplified.
- Revenue protected or increased.
- Risk reduced.
- Proposed price.

Pass condition:

- The customer can see value worth at least twice the price.
- Pricing matches the customer's budget reality.

Fail signals:

- No one can explain the ROI.
- Price is based on how hard it is to build, not customer value.
- The customer likes it but has no reason to pay now.

### 3. Will they keep paying?

Prefer:

- High-frequency needs.
- Standardized workflows.
- Repeatable delivery.
- Low marginal cost.
- Data or content that refreshes regularly.

Avoid as first product:

- One-off custom consulting.
- Heavy per-customer configuration.
- Low-frequency novelty.
- Workflows that require manual rescue every time.

Pass condition:

- Usage repeats weekly or monthly.
- Each new customer does not require rebuilding the product.
- The product improves or becomes stickier with usage.

Fail signals:

- Every sale needs custom development.
- The product is only useful once.
- Customer success depends on the founder manually operating behind the scenes forever.

## Verdict Labels

Use one:

- `GO`: proceed to PRD and MVP planning.
- `NARROW`: promising, but narrow customer/scope first.
- `VALIDATE`: do interviews, price testing, or manual pilot before docs/code.
- `STOP`: likely toy, art piece, or unscalable custom work.

## Required Final Line

End the gate with:

```text
The single most important thing to validate next is: ...
```
